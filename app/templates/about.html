About This Project

Brain Tumor MRI Classification

This web application uses deep learning to classify brain tumor MRI scans into four categories: Glioma, Meningioma, Pituitary tumor, or No tumor.

Brain Tumor Types
Glioma

A tumor that starts in the glial cells of the brain or the spine. Glial cells are the cells that surround and support neurons. Gliomas comprise about 30% of all brain tumors and central nervous system tumors, and 80% of all malignant brain tumors.

Meningioma

A tumor that forms on the membranes that cover the brain and spinal cord just inside the skull. These tumors are usually benign (not cancerous). They occur more commonly in women and account for about 30% of all brain tumors.

Pituitary Tumor

A growth that develops in the pituitary gland, which is a small, bean-shaped gland situated at the base of the brain. Most pituitary tumors are benign and don't spread to other parts of the body. However, they can cause the pituitary gland to produce either too many or too few hormones.

Technology Used
  • Deep Learning: MobileNetV2 CNN architecture
  • Backend: Python, Flask, TensorFlow, Keras
  • Frontend: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap
  • Dataset: Over 7,000 MRI images across four categories
  • Training: Transfer learning with fine-tuning
